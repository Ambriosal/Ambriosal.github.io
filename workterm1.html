IT Assistant

UoG | Research Service Office

Summer 2024

This was my first co-op position! I initially worked here during the Winter 2024 semester as a Work-Study student. I was then onboarded as a full-time co-op student the following summer, where I took on the role of IT Assistant, managing web content and overseeing migration tasks.

I worked in the IT department of the Research Service Office of the University of Guelph. This office is a key research hub, partnering with various colleges, the Ontario Agri-Food Innovation Alliance, and the Ontario Government. It serves as a central point for scientists, researchers, students, and organizations seeking research processing and approval. In the IT department, we handled all things tech—from maintaining database records and automating processes to managing the office’s website.

My title was Information Technology Assistant. A major responsibility in this role was web content management. When requested by coworkers, I made necessary modifications to the office's website using Drupal, HTML, CSS, and other web development technologies. I was also responsible for ensuring that all published online content complied with AODA and WCAG requirements. In addition to web content management, I provided technical support for staff, including assistance with hybrid meetings, PDF form creation, and the use of Microsoft Office products. Another key duty was maintaining the operational integrity of internal email records stored in the office's database.

Work

During my co-op position, I embraced a hybrid work model, spending two days a week in the office and the rest working remotely. My primary role involved managing web update requests from various divisions within the office. Given my background in web development technologies like HTML and CSS, I adapted quickly to using Drupal, the content management system we relied on. My tasks included ensuring all content met AODA requirements, which added an extra layer of detail and care to my work.

One of the most significant projects I was involved in was transforming a cumbersome 75-page manual process into a sleek, online form for the Human Ethics department. This was a cross-departmental effort involving over a dozen team members from different offices. My role was to integrate all the web content about the new system, which required meticulous attention to detail and collaboration.

Another critical responsibility was managing the office's email records database, which contained over 4,000 records spanning a decade. I was tasked with ensuring the records were processed correctly and creating reports on defective records to identify and resolve issues. Additionally, I played a key role in migrating our content management system from Drupal to Backdrop. This transition involved creating suitable alternatives for missing components in Backdrop and ensuring configurations were consistent between the old and new systems.

People

The people I worked with made my experience truly memorable. Despite the office often being quiet due to many employees working from home, I was able to forge meaningful connections with my colleagues. My supervisor, Tom Crabtree, and my mentor, Andrew Burke, were incredibly supportive and knowledgeable. The IT department, a tight-knit team of three, provided invaluable guidance and camaraderie. Their support was instrumental in my professional growth over the eight months.

My contributions ranged from day-to-day tasks like managing web content and ensuring AODA compliance to significant projects such as the internal form automation and content migration. Each task, whether it involved technical challenges or collaborative efforts, allowed me to apply and expand my skills. The experience of working on diverse projects, from web content management to complex database maintenance, was both challenging and rewarding.
